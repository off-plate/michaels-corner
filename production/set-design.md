# Set Design Reference

The locked-in setup for talking-head video. Same scene shot two ways: portrait (9:16) for Shorts/Reels/TikTok and landscape (16:9) for YouTube long-form.

---

## Scene overview (both formats)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Scene** | Interview / conversation |
| **Shot type** | Medium close-up (MCU) |
| **Angle** | 3/4 profile — subject angled 30°–45° away from camera |
| **Framing** | Half body, chest-up |
| **Focus** | Subject in focus, background slightly blurred |
| **Purpose** | Natural conversational look, engages off-camera interviewer |

---

## Camera settings

| | |
|---|---|
| **Focal length** | 50mm – 85mm |
| **Aperture** | f/2.0 – f/2.8 |
| **Focus mode** | AF single point — locked on eyes |
| **Resolution** | 4K |
| **Frame rate** | 24fps or 25fps |
| **White balance** | 5000K – 5600K |
| **ISO** | 100 – 400 (adjust to light) |

---

## Camera position (top view)

```
        BACKGROUND
        ──────────────
              🌿
        SUBJECT
            \
             \ 30°–45°
              \
             📷 CAMERA
```

Subject sits facing 30°–45° away from camera. Looking at off-camera interviewer (not into the lens) for the conversational feel.

---

## Lighting setup (3-point)

```
    KEY LIGHT                  FILL LIGHT
       ╲ 45°                       20°–30° ╱
        ╲                                 ╱
         ╲      SUBJECT                  ╱
                   ↓
              BACK LIGHT
```

- **Key light**: 45° angle from subject (primary)
- **Fill light**: opposite side, lower intensity (softens shadows)
- **Back light**: subtle rim on shoulders / hair (separates from background)
- **Background light** (optional): practical lamp adds depth

---

## Set design & background

- Keep background clean and non-distracting
- Add depth with practical lights (lamp, shelf, etc.)
- Neutral or warm tones
- Maintain background blur (bokeh) via wide aperture

**Visible elements**: floor lamp (warm practical), open shelving with a few props (plant, small objects), neutral wall.

**Wardrobe**: solid-color knit or shirt that contrasts the background. The reference look uses a deep red zip-knit.

---

## Composition guide

- Subject on one side of the frame (rule of thirds)
- Leave negative space on the opposite side for text / graphics overlays
- Avoid centering
- Keep posture open and natural

### Portrait (9:16) specific
- Subject occupies ~60% of vertical frame
- Head near upper third, hands visible at lower third
- Negative space typically left side (for burned-in captions / titles)

### Landscape (16:9) specific
- Subject on left or right third
- More breathing room on opposite side for chyrons / lower-thirds

---

## Final checklist (before rolling)

- [ ] Half body in frame (chest-up)
- [ ] Subject angled 30°–45° away from camera
- [ ] Looking at off-camera interviewer (not into lens)
- [ ] Face and body in focus
- [ ] Background slightly blurred
- [ ] Lighting balanced — no harsh shadows on face
- [ ] Natural expression, open posture
- [ ] Practical light visible in background for depth
